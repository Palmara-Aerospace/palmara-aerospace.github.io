Our Story

Founded in March of 2021 in Coventry, England by Malik Abdulla, an Aerospace Engineering student, Palmara set out on a journey to revolutionise the drone market by combining modern technological breakthroughs with the miracle that is flight.

Despite the hardships we've encountered along the way and the challenges presented as a result of breaking into the complex aerospace industry as a self-funded start-up, we've made breathtaking strides towards building a modular platform for our imaging drones.

As the UK's first and currently only end-to-end producer of drone technology, we aim to set the bar high by producing our first functioning prototype within 12 months of founding.

By combining open-source flight software with AI modules and state of the art components, we're working towards developing the worlds first multi-spectrum completely autonomous range of drones.

  • March 2021

    Humble Beginnings

    On a cold March night in an empty train station, what started as an idea for an aerospace consulting and research firm was developed. This idea lead to the spark the resulted in the creation of the UK's first and currently only end-to-end producer of aerial imaging drone technology.

  • May 2021

    A Company Is Born

    After three months of research and planning in a living room in Coventry, England, Palmara began its journey after being formally incorporated in the United Kingdom on the 20th of May.

  • August 2021

    Phase One: Start-up

    On the 7th of July, we began converting a for rent office in Birmingham, England, into our first R&D centre. Stocked with two computers, a 3D printer and enough spool to stretch to Wales, we began work on our initial designs and sketches.

  • November 2021

    Phase Two: Change

    Realising that it was neither practical nor cost-effective to run two offices AND an R&D centre, we made the difficult decision to pack up and consolidate our business under one roof. On the 18th of September, the company and its founder moved to London in pursuit of this dream.

  • January 2022

    Phase Three: Prototype

    Here at Palmara, we acknowledge that competent management is a hallmark of successful business, but there can't be a business without a product or service. In this next step in our evolution, we sketch, design, and incorporate a series of world-changing technologies to shape our product lines.

    From 3D printed parts to open-source flight software and AI processes run on state of the art components, our primary focus is now producing a viable prototype capable of integrating these technologies into a single compact platform.

  • May 2022

    Phase Four: Licensing

    To ensure public safety and legal compliance, we plan to file for our first commercial drone licence after the development of the Palmara One prototype.

  • July 2022

    Phase Four: Public Test

    This milestone is important to us for many reasons. It serves as proof of our ability to meet deadlines and deliver on projects in a timely manner.

    It would also make our company one of the fastest to achieve stable flight (in under 12 months!), and the only privately-funded British small business to achieve stable flight.
